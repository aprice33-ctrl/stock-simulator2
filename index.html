Executive Summary: What Changed in the Revised Model

The previous version was useful for teaching company-level stock selection, but it treated price movement as mostly random company news. The revised version now embeds macroeconomic context into valuation and gameplay.

  • Added market-cycle states: expansion, high-rate environment, recession, recovery, and earnings boom now change the investment setting.
  • Added interest-rate mechanics: interest rates directly change the required return, which changes DCF/Gordon Growth valuation estimates.
  • Added discount-rate sensitivity: high-growth stocks react more strongly when required returns rise because more of their value depends on future cash flows.
  • Added recession effects: recessions reduce growth expectations, pressure cyclical companies, and make safer dividend/income stocks relatively more attractive.
  • Added earnings-season effects: strong or weak earnings adjust expected growth and valuation instead of moving prices randomly.
  • Improved Chapter 8 alignment: the game now shows how dividend valuation, required return, growth, benchmark multiples, and risk interact in one system.

Game Log

DCF / Dividend Logic

Chapter 8 shows stock value as the present value of expected future cash flows. In this game, rising interest rates increase required return and reduce model values.

Market Cycle Logic

Expansion raises growth expectations; recession reduces cash-flow expectations; recovery improves sentiment; high-rate environments pressure growth stocks.

Earnings Season Logic

Strong earnings boost expected growth and model value. Weak earnings reduce growth assumptions and can make a stock look overvalued.
